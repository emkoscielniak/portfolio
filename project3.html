Diary Studies Report

Understanding media consumption behavior through qualitative research

Role UX Researcher
Timeline 4 Weeks
Tools Miro, Google Forms, Notion
UX Research Qualitative Analysis Thematic Analysis Affinity Mapping Empathy Map

Project Overview

This individual research study utilized a 7-day diary method with 2 participants to capture the context of user media consumption in the moment. The project demonstrates advanced qualitative research skills, from study design through data synthesis and presentation.

By capturing real-time insights into how, when, and why people consume media, this study reveals nuanced patterns that traditional retrospective interviews might miss.

Research Design

I designed a longitudinal diary study to understand media consumption behavior in natural contexts over time.

Research Questions

  • What types of media do people consume throughout their day?
  • What contextual factors influence media choices?
  • How do media consumption patterns vary across different situations?
  • What emotional and social needs does media consumption fulfill?

Methodology

Participants were asked to log each media consumption instance over 7 days, recording:

  • Type of media (video, music, social media, news, etc.)
  • Platform or source
  • Duration and time of day
  • Physical location and social context
  • Emotional state before and after
  • Reason for consuming that particular media

Data Collection

2 Participants
7 Days
89 Total Entries

I created a simple, mobile-friendly form to minimize friction in data collection. Daily reminder notifications helped maintain participation consistency throughout the week.

User-friendly diary entry form designed for quick, in-the-moment logging

Qualitative Analysis

I employed multiple qualitative analysis techniques to extract meaningful insights from the diary data:

Affinity Mapping

I organized all diary entries into groups based on emerging patterns and themes. This bottom-up approach revealed unexpected connections between different media consumption behaviors.

Digital affinity map showing clustered themes and patterns

Thematic Analysis

Through iterative coding, I identified recurring themes across participants:

  • Routine & Ritual: Morning news, lunch break entertainment, bedtime wind-down
  • Emotional Regulation: Using media to manage stress, boredom, or anxiety
  • Social Connection: Staying connected through shared content
  • Background Noise: Media as ambient company during tasks
  • Learning & Growth: Educational content and skill development

Empathy Mapping

I synthesized findings into empathy maps to capture participants' thoughts, feelings, actions, and pain points around media consumption.

Empathy map revealing user motivations and frustrations

Key Findings

Context is King

Media choices are heavily influenced by time of day, location, and social setting

Multitasking Reality

Most media consumption happens alongside other activities, not as primary focus

Emotional Triggers

Specific emotional states predictably lead to certain media consumption patterns

Platform Switching

Users fluidly move between platforms depending on device availability and privacy needs

Behavioral Patterns

  • Morning media tends to be informational (news, weather, email)
  • Mid-day consumption is often social (messaging, social media)
  • Evening media leans toward entertainment and relaxation
  • Weekend patterns differ significantly from weekdays
  • Alone time favors deeper content engagement

Design Implications

These findings suggest several opportunities for media platform design:

Recommendations

  • Context-aware content: Adapt recommendations based on time, location, and user state
  • Flexible engagement modes: Support both focused and background consumption
  • Emotional intelligence: Recognize and respond to user mood and needs
  • Seamless transitions: Enable smooth handoffs between devices and platforms
  • Routine building: Help users establish healthy media habits

Methodology Reflection

Conducting this diary study taught me valuable lessons about qualitative research:

Strengths of Diary Studies

  • Captures in-the-moment experiences, reducing recall bias
  • Reveals patterns over time that single-session research might miss
  • Provides rich contextual data about real-world behavior
  • Less researcher presence reduces observer effect

Challenges & Solutions

  • Participation fatigue: Kept forms short and sent encouraging reminders
  • Data volume: Used systematic coding and analysis frameworks
  • Incomplete entries: Followed up with clarifying questions
  • Synthesis complexity: Employed multiple analysis methods for triangulation

Outcomes & Learnings

This project demonstrated my ability to independently design, conduct, and analyze qualitative research. The final deliverable was a comprehensive research report that synthesized complex behavioral data into clear, actionable insights.

Skills Developed

  • Research design: Creating effective longitudinal study protocols
  • Data analysis: Applying multiple qualitative analysis methods
  • Pattern recognition: Identifying meaningful themes in unstructured data
  • Synthesis & communication: Presenting findings in accessible formats
  • Ethical research: Maintaining participant privacy and data security

Applications

The insights from this study could inform:

  • Content recommendation algorithm improvements
  • Platform feature prioritization
  • User interface design decisions
  • Content creator strategy
  • Digital wellness features
